It looks at the last 24 months of data for a country and feeds that sequence through an LSTM which learns temporal patterns like escalation trends and seasonal cycles.
Separately it reads slow-moving country characteristics like democracy score and military regime type, and combines those with the sequence output.
From that combined representation it produces three numbers that together define a full probability distribution:

μ — the expected fatality count
α — how spread out / uncertain the distribution is
π — the probability that this month is genuine peace with zero fatalities

Instead of running the model once, it runs 50 times with dropout randomly switching off different neurons each time. The variation across those 50 runs tells you epistemic uncertainty — how confident the model is based on what it has seen in training. The spread of the ZINB distribution itself tells you aleatoric uncertainty
 — how inherently unpredictable conflict is in that country-month regardless of the model.
